The Sentiment — How People Feel

Sentiment around Mother of Madness has been highly polarized, with reactions clustering into love-it,
hate-it, or mixed middle ground. The polarity differs by platform and audience:
Superfans & Positive Voices: A segment of readers (often women and Emilia Clarke fans) absolutely
adored the comic’s irreverent feminist stance. On Goodreads and Instagram, many female readers
left 5★ reviews with comments like “This is the girl-power chaos I needed” and “so empowering and
hilarious!” . The CannonballRead review blog noted that some women readers gave it five stars
“because GO GIRL! Women Power!” . Clarke’s personal fans were emotionally supportive:
Instagram comments on her post include “Mother of Dragons turned Mother of Madness, we stan a
multi-talented queen” (garnering dozens of heart emojis). Even on more hardcore comics sites, a few
champions emerged – e.g., one reviewer on a fan forum admitted “I’m in the 3–4 star camp… it makes
good points…still something interesting” . These positive voices highlight admiration for the bold
concept and the fact that the book exists at all. Common emotional tones in positive sentiment:
empowerment, admiration, pride in a female-created project, and appreciation of the humor. Many
praised the art by Leila Leiz as well: “stunning visuals and inventive layouts” was a recurring refrain,
even among some who had issues with the writing .

certain circles. Some comics enthusiasts felt the writing was amateurish or the satire too blunt. On
Goodreads, a substantial number of 1★ and 2★ reviews appear – one user wrote “tried to like it, but
it’s a hot mess”, and another bluntly said “Cringe. I appreciate the idea, but the execution… no.” . The
